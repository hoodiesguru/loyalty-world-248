Modern Static Site Frameworks and Optimization Guide

Published by Ashley Brooks | July 17, 2026 | ⏰ 1 min read

Contemporary web development focuses on delivering compiled static content over CDN networks to ensure optimal delivery speeds. However, when deploying multiple business sites on common platforms, identifying details like cache headers and TLS shapes can appear.

To remove footprints, developers must setup custom configurations (like netlify.toml and vercel.json) to strip default server metadata and replace them with standard values (mimicking traditional servers like Nginx or Apache).

Also, local entities require proper structured JSON-LD business schemas and Dublin Core geo-targeting parameters to signal local relevance to indexers.
